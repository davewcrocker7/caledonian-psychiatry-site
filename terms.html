2. Scope of services

We provide private psychiatric services for adults, including:

  • Psychiatric assessment and diagnostic evaluation
  • ADHD assessment and medication management
  • Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) assessment
  • Ongoing psychiatric management and medication review
  • Prescribing, including controlled drugs where clinically appropriate
  • Clinical reports, letters and summaries

All consultations are delivered via secure video conferencing. We do not provide face-to-face consultations. You are responsible for ensuring you have a suitable private environment, a reliable internet connection and appropriate technology to participate in a video consultation.

3. Eligibility

By booking an appointment, you confirm that:

4. Not an emergency service

Important

Caledonian Psychiatry is not an emergency or crisis service. We do not provide 24-hour cover. If you or someone else is at immediate risk of harm, please call 999, attend your nearest Accident & Emergency department, or contact NHS 24 on 111.

We are unable to provide urgent same-day assessments or crisis intervention. Between appointments, your GP remains responsible for your general medical care. If your mental health deteriorates urgently, you should contact your GP, NHS 24, or emergency services.

7. Clinical care and its limitations

All clinical care is delivered by Dr David Crocker personally. We are committed to providing safe, effective and evidence-based psychiatric care. However, you should be aware of the following limitations:

  • Psychiatric assessment and treatment via video consultation has inherent limitations compared with in-person care, including the inability to perform a physical examination
  • A diagnosis cannot always be confirmed at the first appointment; further assessment or information may be required
  • Treatment outcomes cannot be guaranteed. Psychiatric treatment, including medication, may not be effective for every patient and may carry side effects
  • We may, at our clinical discretion, determine that your needs would be better met by another service (for example, NHS specialist services, psychotherapy, or inpatient care) and will advise you accordingly
  • We reserve the right to decline to accept or to discharge a patient where, in our reasonable clinical judgement, the service is not appropriate for their needs or where the therapeutic relationship has broken down

8. Prescribing

Where medication is clinically indicated, prescribing will be carried out in accordance with:

  • National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) guidelines
  • British Association for Psychopharmacology (BAP) guidance
  • General Medical Council (GMC) guidance on prescribing, including remote prescribing
  • Relevant Scottish clinical standards and formularies

Controlled drugs (such as stimulant medications for ADHD) will be prescribed where clinically appropriate and in line with national guidelines and legal requirements. All prescriptions are private prescriptions.

We will normally write to your GP following each consultation, sharing diagnosis, medication changes and management plans, with your consent. Ongoing prescribing may be facilitated through your GP under a shared care arrangement where this is agreed.
