POL-00483 — Patient Feedback and Complaints Policy

Our commitment

Caledonian Psychiatry is the trading name of Edinburgh Psych Limited. We are committed to providing a high standard of care and service to all patients. We take all complaints and concerns seriously and use them as an opportunity to learn and improve.

This policy sets out how you can raise a concern or make a complaint about any aspect of the service you have received. It has been developed in accordance with the requirements of Healthcare Improvement Scotland (HIS) and the Health and Social Care Standards.

The Registered Manager responsible for handling complaints is Dr David Crocker.

Who can make a complaint

A complaint can be made by:

  • A patient who is receiving or has received care from Caledonian Psychiatry
  • A representative acting on behalf of a patient, with their consent
  • A relative, carer or other person who has been affected by the service

Where a complaint is made on behalf of someone else, we may need to confirm that the person has given their consent before we can share information about their care.

How to raise a concern or complaint

You may raise a concern or complaint verbally (by telephone) or in writing (by email or post). There is no requirement to put your complaint in writing at the initial stage.

We encourage you to raise any concerns as soon as possible. Whilst there is no strict time limit, it is generally easier to investigate matters if they are raised within 12 months of the event in question.

Our complaints process

We follow a three-stage process to ensure that all concerns are dealt with fairly, thoroughly and promptly.

Stage 1: Informal resolution

Many concerns can be resolved quickly through direct discussion. If you are unhappy with any aspect of your care, we encourage you to raise this directly with Dr Crocker at the earliest opportunity. We will do our best to resolve the matter informally and to your satisfaction.

This may involve a telephone conversation, a discussion at your next appointment, or a brief written response by email.

Stage 2: Formal written complaint

If you are not satisfied with the outcome of Stage 1, or if you prefer to make a formal complaint from the outset, you may do so in writing by email or by post using the contact details above.

When making a formal complaint, it is helpful to include:

  • Your full name and contact details
  • A description of your concern and what happened
  • When the event occurred
  • The outcome you are seeking

We will:

  • Acknowledge your complaint within 3 working days of receipt
  • Investigate the matter thoroughly and impartially
  • Provide a full written response within 20 working days of receipt

If we are unable to complete our investigation within 20 working days, we will write to you to explain the reason for the delay and provide a revised timescale.

Our response will include a summary of the complaint, our findings, any actions we have taken or intend to take, and information about how to escalate the matter further if you remain dissatisfied.

Stage 3: External escalation

If you are not satisfied with the outcome of our internal complaints process, you have the right to escalate your complaint to an external body. The appropriate organisation will depend on the nature of your concern.

How we handle complaints

All complaints, whether informal or formal, are treated with confidentiality and respect. We will not discriminate against any patient who raises a complaint, and making a complaint will not affect the care you receive.

Our approach to complaints handling includes the following principles:

  • Fairness: All complaints are investigated impartially and without prejudice.
  • Thoroughness: We examine all relevant information and, where appropriate, seek independent advice.
  • Transparency: We provide clear and honest responses, including acknowledgement of any failings identified.
  • Timeliness: We respond within the stated timeframes and keep you informed of progress.
  • Learning: We use complaints as an opportunity to improve our service.

Record keeping and learning

All complaints are logged in a confidential complaints register, recording the nature of the complaint, the investigation undertaken, the outcome and any actions taken. This register is reviewed regularly to identify trends, themes and areas for improvement.

Where a complaint identifies a need for change, we will implement appropriate improvements to our policies, procedures or clinical practice. Significant findings are incorporated into our ongoing quality improvement processes in line with HIS requirements.

Support and advocacy

If you need support in making a complaint, you may find it helpful to contact an independent advocacy service. In Scotland, you can find local advocacy services through the Scottish Independent Advocacy Alliance (SIAA).
